Introduction


Sinharaja Forest Reserve is located 300-1170 m southwest of the central hills of Sri Lanka bordering the Galle District and covers an area of 11,250 ha in the Rakwana area. It has received international attention and has been declared a Biosphere Reserve and a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. Part of the lowland rainforest ecosystem of Sri Lanka, this mountainous rainforest has been protected from commercialization due to its inaccessibility. Another reason for this is that it was declared a Biosphere Reserve in 1607 and a World Heritage Site in 1988. The name Sinharaja means the sinharajadahana.
